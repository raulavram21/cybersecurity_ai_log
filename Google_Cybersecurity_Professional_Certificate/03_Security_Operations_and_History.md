Security Operations and History


Historical Catalysts

    The Morris Worm (1988): An early, highly disruptive malware worm. (Context: Its widespread impact proved automated threats could cripple networks, leading directly to the creation of formal incident response teams.)

    Melissa (1999) & Blaster (2003): Early examples of massive financial damage caused by macro-viruses and self-replicating OS exploits spreading without human intervention.

Incident Response

    CSIRT (Computer Security Incident Response Team): Specialized groups formed to manage and respond to computer security emergencies.

    Playbooks: Documented, step-by-step instructions guiding analysts on exactly how to investigate specific alerts. (Context: Eliminates guesswork during high-pressure incidents.)

    Digital Forensics: Identifying, analyzing, and legally preserving criminal evidence within digital networks post-breach.
