Threat Landscape and Actors


Threat Actor Profiles

    Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs): Highly skilled attackers who gain unauthorized network access and remain undetected for long periods. (Context: Often state-sponsored, targeting critical infrastructure or intellectual property.)

    Insider Threats: Individuals who abuse authorized system access to obtain data or cause harm. (Context: Highly dangerous because they already bypass external perimeter defenses; can be malicious or accidental.)

    Hacktivists: Threat actors driven primarily by a political or social agenda. (Context: They execute attacks for demonstrations, propaganda, or social change rather than financial profit.)

Attack Vectors & Domain Mapping

    Phishing & Social Engineering: Manipulating human trust (Authority, Scarcity, Urgency) to steal data. Includes BEC (Business Email Compromise), Spear Phishing, Whaling, Vishing (voice), Smishing (SMS), and USB Baiting. (Domain: Security and Risk Management)

    Malware: * Viruses: Malicious code that damages data. (Context: Requires user interaction to execute.)

        Worms: Self-replicating malware. (Context: Spreads automatically across networks without user interaction.)

        Ransomware: Encrypts data and demands payment for the decryption key.

        Spyware: Secretly gathers and sells user information.

    Password Attacks: Attempting to breach credentials using methods like Brute Force or Rainbow Tables. (Domain: Communication and Network Security)

    Physical Attacks: Security incidents involving physical environments, like malicious cables or card skimming. (Domain: Asset Security)

    Supply-Chain Attacks: Targeting vulnerable components within third-party vendor pipelines before they reach the consumer. (Domains: Risk Management, Architecture, Operations)

    Adversarial AI: Manipulating AI/ML models to execute attacks more efficiently. (Domains: Network Security, IAM)
